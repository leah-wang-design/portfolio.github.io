WORK ABOUT

Buywithme Online Shopping App

An online shopping app that helps users get better deals by connecting with other people.

Background

Buywithme is an e-commerce startup that helps users get better deals by connecting people with their friends and families with a fun browsing experience. The MVP will launch in mid-July with beauty and personal care products and be available as a mobile web app.

I worked closely with the founder, PM, designers to validate the current design and make revisions to improve the user experience. I conducted user interviews and design audits to empathize with the user and gain insight. I also revised designs by conducting wireframes, style guides, and high-fidelity prototypes.

Problem

Buywithme requires users to 'team-up' with others to get better deals. So how do we help users better understand the concept of 'team-buying' and encourage them to buy more products within the app?

Process

  • Understand the Problems

    • As the first step, we designed a screener survey and recruited potential users to conduct 1-on-1 interviews. By synthesizing the results we collected through the ten interviews, we learned that though general user flow was easy to understand, it lacks some basic information and some components were confusing.

  • Personas

    • Through stakeholder interviews and research the team has conducted before, we learned two types of people are the current target users for the app: Busy Mom Kelly and Social Student Amy.

    personas


  • Interviews

    • With the two personas in mind, we sent questionnaires to recruit target users for user testing. The target users, ages 19-45, often buy personal care or beauty products online with smartphones for themselves or families, with the primary purposes of obtaining more choices for the products and getting discounts easier. They are also open to making purchases based on recommendations and would like to recommend products to others.

    Interview Quotes


  • Affinity Map

    • With all the information we gathered, we conducted the affinity map to gain insights. We found the app is easy to navigate in general, and the users can understand the concept quickly through the short explanations, illustrations, and icons. However, there are some parts of the design confusing and could be problematic for the users.

  • Research Insights

    Insights
  • Design Iterations

    • After knowing what to explore for each page, I started sketching out some ideas and brainstorming ideas with the team. Once we had a general direction, I began to conduct low-fidelity wireframes in Figma.

  • Explore Product Displays

    • The product image occupies the full-width page in the original design and doesn't have a filter or search feature, making it challenging to browse fast. The picture of the products also auto-switch per 3 seconds - many users pointed out it's nerve-wracking. So I explored multiple options to display the product cards and added ratings for the products.

    Wireframes: Discover Page/Homepage(click to enlarge)


  • Replace the Interactive Button

    • The original design lacks basic information to explain the different prices on the page, and it dedicated a big chunk of space to the interactive component. However, we learned that the users thought the element was unnecessary and were confused by its existence through user testing. So I designed different options to have a better hierarchy for the information on the page. In addition, I added a 'like' button with the number of people who liked the products to allow users to save products easily and encourage users to buy the products.

    Wireframes: Product Detail Page(click to enlarge)
  • Clarify the Team

    • I redesigned the 'my team' page to show more information about the teammates to the users and allow the users to take actions to form or restart the team.



    Wireframes: Product Detail Page(click to enlarge)

    • After team discussion and talking with stakeholders, I also suggested changing the old design systems to make the icons more accurate and altered the color palette to make the entire color system more pleasing. With the modified design system, we designed high-fidelity interfaces.

  • Design Validation

    • After presenting the high-fidelity pages to the stakeholders, the CEO wasn't convinced about the new design for the homepage, which displays the products in smaller cards. So we decided to conduct AB testing with the two options, one with larger product cards, one with smaller ones. The version with smaller cards received a 4.9/7 satisfaction rate based on user testing compared to 4.5/7 received for the other version.

  • Reflections

    • Working on the online shopping app through collaborating with the founder, PMs and designers was a great experience. I was able to talk to the users to gain insights, revise the current design system, and improve the app's user experience.

    Due to the limited time, we only tested the design that makes the most significant impact. But looking back, I wish I could have more time designing the 'order' page and conducting user testing to validate the design during the entire process.
