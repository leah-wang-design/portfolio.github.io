WORK ABOUT

Moodie Music Player

A music app that allows you to listen to music by your mood, react to the music with mood and classify the songs into moods.

Background

'Moodie' is a project I worked on as a designer for a UI course. The project aims to design a music player and solve a problem we encountered in daily life.

Problem

How might we enable users to find and organize music based on their emotions quickly?

Role

UX Research, UX Design, UI Design, Interaction Design, Prototype

Team

Personal Project

Tools

Figma, Principle

Moodie Homepage

Pick the song according to your choice of mood

Find music based on their needs in an intuitive way, through categorizing music based on daily moods.

Moodie Music Player

React to the song based on your choice of mood

Quickly add songs to the playlist by reacting to the song. Interact with other users by checking out the comment sections.

Moodie My Account

Find your customized playlists and mood records

Playlists have both the ones you created and "Mood list" that auto-generated from the reactions you made to the songs.

Process

  • Research

    • I began the process by interviewing friends on their experience with current music app they are using and painpoints they have. I went through the user interactions within the major music apps (spotify,apple music, youtube music, soundcloud) to get a better understanding of the products.

  • Competitive Study

    • Music is an integral part of human life, but at the same time, that is not the sphere where people would like to make any additional efforts. Everything the user needs here is clear navigation and fast work. However, considering the number of competitors in this market, itâ€™s important to think about something original in design so that the service can stand out from the crowd. I did a competitive study for the major music players in the market, in order to get a sense of what kind of functions exist and the potentials to explore out of music app.

    For music players like Spotify, Youtube Music, Apple Music, Amazon Prime Music and Tidal, though the general services they provide are all similar (includes Explore music, personalized recommendation, search music, add music to playlist, customize playlist), they all have their own star features that help them stand out in the market, such as strong searching options, options to show lyrics, integration with youtube, integration with Alexa, and options to choose music quality and find new songs based on music genres.

  • Understand the Users

    • In order to get a better understanding of the users, I looked through comments in app store for the major music apps above. And I interviewed six friends who are daily users of apple music, spotify or youtube music.

    Some quotes from the users


    Based on the research, I found 'finding music' and 'organizing music' are the most common complaints from the users. Users found it sometimes challenging to find the ones they want to listen to, and it's a pain to organize the playlists if they have a lot of songs.

  • Define the Problem

    • So how do we help users find music and organize music easily while making it fun and stand out from other music apps?

    I created a persona to ensure the target audience is clear for the app-Eleanor is a music lover. She likes traveling, going to music festivals, and making friends. She's motivated to use apps to find new songs and interact with people who share similar music tastes with her.

    Persona


  • Ideation

    • By sketching mindmap, I started to brainstorm as many as ideas I can, and pick the solutions that are more innovative and potentials to be developed further.

    One idea I came up with is using 'mood' to label the song and categorize the song to different playlists. In order to validate the idea, I conducted research on the major music apps, and found 'genre' and 'mood' currently are the most important influencers when choosing music to listen to, yet the terms to define 'mood'and 'genre' are often mixed, sometimes can be ambiguous and misleading. As a starting point, I referred to the report Tufts University did to classify the music mood, and came up with 10 moods to label the music.

  • Design and Iterations

    • By applying IOS design guide, I took one step further from the sketch version of wireframe to get to a high-fidelity wireframe. I conducted more usability tests with real users, and iterated different versions of necessary tabs and icon sizes.

  • UI KIT

    • High-Fidelity

    In my first iterations, I tried to optimize the interactivity of the app by experimenting with different locations for the 'mood' choice bar, adjusting color contrast to better pop the 'mood' features, as well as maintaining a consistent use of colors throughout each page of the app.

  • Prototype and Testing

    • I interviewed five users with this prototype and gained some valuable feedback. They thought the idea of discovering music based on emotion is fun and interesting. They love the fact that they can see the recommendations based on the mood they choose. I also got some feedback on the comment section that they want to click on other users to see their playlists.
